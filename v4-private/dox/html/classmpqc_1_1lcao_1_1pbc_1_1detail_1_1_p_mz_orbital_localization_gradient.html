Detailed Description

template<typename Tile, typename Policy>
class mpqc::lcao::pbc::detail::PMzOrbitalLocalizationGradient< Tile, Policy >

Finds a stationary point of the PM objective function for a periodic systems.

PM functional is defined as

\[ \sum_{\bf R}^{\rm cells} \sum_A^{\rm atoms} \sum_i | Q^{A_{\bf R}}_i |^p , \]

where \( i \) denotes an orbital in the input set, \( {\bf R} \) denotes position vector of a unit cell in the localization range (typically determined by the range of \( {\bf k} \) points), \( A \) denotes an atom in a unit cell; thus, \( A_{\bf R} \) denotes atom \( A \) in the unit cell defined by \( {\bf R} \). \( Q^{A_{\bf R}}_i \) is the charge created by orbital \( i \) on atom \( A_{\bf R} \); we use pseudoinverse Mulliken charges in a measuring (typically, minimal) AO basis, defined as

\[ Q^{A_{\bf R}}_i = \sum_{\bar{\mu}_{A_{\bf R}}} \bar{C}^i_{\bar{\mu}_{A_{\bf R}}} \langle \bar{\mu}_{A_{\bf R}} | \psi_i \rangle \]

where \( \bar{\mu}_{A_{\bf R}} \) are the measuring basis AOs on atom \( A_{\bf R} \) , \( \psi_i \) are the input orbitals , and \( \bar{C} \) are the coefficients of input orbitals projected on the measuring AO basis by pseudoinverse method. To be continued ...
