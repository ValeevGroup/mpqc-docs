Detailed Description

template<typename Tile, typename Policy>
class mpqc::lcao::FockBuilder< Tile, Policy >

FockBuilder computes the (2-e part) of the Fock matrix using either the (1-particle) density matrix or the occupied orbital AO coefficients.

computes the 2-e part of the Fock matrix and the corresponding energy contributions (and their geometric derivatives, if needed)

Parameters
Dthe (1-particle) density matrix in Pauli representation
Cthe occupied orbital AO coefficient matrix
target_precisionthe (absolute) target precision of the Fock matrix; this precision is not in general guaranteed, but the actual precision of the Fock matrix is usually proportional to this value.
deriv_orderthe order of geometric derivatives of the Fock matrix and energy to evaluate; the default is 0, i.e. compute standard 2-body part of Fock matrix and (if possible) energy
energy_onlyif true, will evaluate the 2-body energy (and its derivatives, if needed); the default is false
Returns
a tuple containing
  • the (possibly evaluated) 2-electron contribution to the Fock matrix, G, in Pauli representation (if deriv_order>0 then the derivatives of the first Pauli component of G appear first, followed by the derivatives of the second component, etc.)
  • the (possibly evaluated) contribution to the 2-body energy that depends quadratically on the density (given as G.dot(D)) and, if needed, its derivatives.
  • the optional contribution to the energy (and its derivatives, if needed) that accounts for non-quadratic dependence of the energy on the density, e.g., the exchange-correlation contribution; This must exclude the quadratic contribution if this builder evaluated the 2-electron contribution to the Fock matrix.
Note
the number of derivatives of order d is (3N)*(3N+1)*...*(3N+d-1)/d!, where N is the number of geometric centers defining the AO basis supporting the density (and/or the Fock matrix). E.g. for N=10 the number of first and second derivatives is 30 and 30*31/2 = 465, respectively. Packing of the derivatives in a sequence occurs according to the CCA convention described here
