Detailed Description

Utilities manipulating spin-specific (meta)data.

Rationale
In spin-collinear (e.g., nonrelativistic) theory alpha and beta spin states are distinct. A pair of data T associated with alpha ( \( m_s=+1/2 \), or spin-up) and beta ( \( m_s=-1/2 \), or spin-down) states will be commonly paired together as a std::pair<T,T>, std::tuple<T,T>, or a TA::DistArrayVector<T>. Examples include Slater determinants represented by a pair {alpha,beta} strings, pairs of {alpha,beta} Fock matrices and 1-RDMs in a nonrelativistic open-shell system, etc. This adds syntactic sugar for dealing with such data pairs using a domain-specific terminology.
Note
A particularly important representation of (vector-space-based) spin-specific data pairs includes the Pauli representation, applicable to e.g. matrices and tensors. Although the Pauli representation can be extended to the spin-non-collinear case, in MPQC it is currently used only to represent spin-collinear quantities. Pauli representation of a spin-collinear matrix O is a sequence:
  • spin-unpolarized case: {(O_alpha + O_beta)/2},
  • spin-polarized case: {(O_alpha + O_beta)/2, (O_alpha - O_beta)/2}.
