Computes Mulliken atomic charges for a nonperiodic system, optionally (use_minbs>0) using the minimal basis to partition the density into atomic contributions.

Parameters
[in]C(occupied) input LCAOs
Returns
the atomic charges
Warning
these are electronic charges, i.e. you want to multiply by occupancy factor (e.g. 2 for closed-shell orbitals) and account for the nuclear charges
Note
The conventional (use_minbs==0) Mulliken charge on atom \( A \) is given by \( P_A = \sum_{\mu \in A} \sum_i \sum_\nu C^i_\nu S^{\nu}_{\mu} C^\mu_i = \sum_{\mu \in A} \sum_\nu P^\mu_\nu S^{\nu}_{\mu} . \) Using a minimal basis, \( \{ \tilde{\mu} \} \) , for columns of density only (use_minbs==1) to make atomic subspaces invariant of the orbital basis, the charges become \( P_A = \sum_{\tilde{\mu} \in A} \sum_i \sum_\nu \left( C^i_{\nu} S^{\nu}_{\tilde{\mu}} C^{\tilde{\mu}}_i \right), \) where we used `‘pseudoinverse’' occupied minimal basis orbitals: \( C_{\tilde{\mu}}^i \equiv \left(\mathbf{\tilde{C}}\right)_{\tilde{\mu}}^i = \left(\left(\mathbf{C} \mathbf{\tilde{S}}\right)^{-1}\right)_{\tilde{\mu}}^i \) with \( \left(\mathbf{C}\right)^i_\mu \equiv C^i_\mu \) , \( \left(\mathbf{\tilde{S}}\right)^\mu_{\tilde{\mu}} \equiv S^\mu_{\tilde{\mu}} \) , and the matrix inverse is defined by the Penrose-Moore pseudoinverse. The pseudoinverse occupied minimal basis orbitals are not orthonormal, but are biorthogonal to \( \mathbf{C} \) : \( \sum_{\mu} \sum_\nu C^i_{\nu} S^{\nu}_{\tilde{\mu}} C^{\tilde{\mu}}_j = \delta^i_j \) . Thus they are covariant to the full-basis counterparts, i.e. linear transformation of \( \mathbf{C} \) accompanied by the inverse transformation of \( \mathbf{\tilde{C}} \) maintains the biorthogonality. Using a minimal basis, \( \{ \tilde{\mu} \} \) , for rows and columns of density (use_minbs==2), the charges become \( P_A = \sum_{\tilde{\mu} \in A} \sum_i \sum_{\tilde{\nu}} \left( C^i_{\tilde{\nu}} S^{\tilde{\nu}}_{\tilde{\mu}} C^{\tilde{\mu}}_i \right), \) where \( C_{\tilde{\nu}}^i \equiv (\mathbf{S}^{-1/2})^{\tilde{\nu}}_{\tilde{\mu}} V^i_{\tilde{\nu}} \) are the molecular orbitals in minimal basis defined with the help of the corresponding occupied orbitals in the orthogonalized minimal basis, \( V^i_{\tilde{\mu}} \) . These are obtained as the right singular vectors with nonzero eigenvalues of the overlap between orthogonal minimal basis orbitals and the occupied orbitals, \( C_i^\mu S^{\tilde{\nu}}_{\mu} (\mathbf{S}^{-1/2})^{\tilde{\mu}}_{\tilde{\nu}} \)

Computes Mulliken atomic charges for a periodic system using the minimal basis for one of the coefficient matrices in the density (minbs=1 )

Parameters
[in]C"canonical" LCAOs
[in]noccnumber of doubly occupied bands (partially occupied bands are not yet supported)
[in]nkthe k point grid size in each dimension
Returns
the atomic charges
Warning
these are electronic charges, i.e. you want to multiply by occupancy factor (e.g. 2 for closed-shell orbitals) and account for the nuclear charges
Note
Only use_minbs==1 case, with atomic domains defined by minimal basis. Mulliken charge on atom \( A \) in the reference cell is given by \( P_A = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{\tilde{\mu} \in A} \sum_{i,\vec{k}} \sum_\nu \left( C^{i,\vec{k}}_{\nu} S^{\nu}_{\tilde{\mu}} C^{\tilde{\mu}}_{i,\vec{k}} + C_{\tilde{\mu}}^{i,\vec{k}} S_{\nu}^{\tilde{\mu}} C_{i,\vec{k}}^{\nu} \right), \) where we used `‘pseudoinverse’' occupied minimal basis orbitals: \( C_{\tilde{\mu}}^{i,\vec{k}} \equiv \left(\mathbf{\tilde{C}}\right)_{\tilde{\mu}}^{i,\vec{k}} = \left(\left(\mathbf{C} \mathbf{\tilde{S}}\right)^{-1}\right)_{\tilde{\mu}}^{i,\vec{k}} \) with \( \left(\mathbf{C}\right)^{i,\vec{k}}_\mu \equiv C^{i,\vec{k}}_\mu \) , \( \left(\mathbf{\tilde{S}}\right)^\mu_{\tilde{\mu}} \equiv S^\mu_{\tilde{\mu}} \) , and the matrix inverse is defined by the Penrose-Moore pseudoinverse. Note that the orbitals are complex, hence \( C^{i,\vec{k}}_\mu = \left( C_{i,\vec{k}}^\mu \right)^* \) The pseudoinverse occupied minimal basis orbitals are not orthonormal, but are biorthogonal to \( \mathbf{C} \) : \( \sum_{\mu} \sum_\nu C^{i,\vec{k}}_{\nu} S^{\nu}_{\tilde{\mu}} C^{\tilde{\mu}}_{j,\vec{k}} = \delta^i_j \) . Thus they are covariant to the full-basis counterparts, i.e. linear transformation of \( \mathbf{C} \) accompanied by the inverse transformation of \( \mathbf{\tilde{C}} \) maintains the biorthogonality.
